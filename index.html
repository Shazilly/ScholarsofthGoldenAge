No. of books written: Over 270

Al-Farabi had great influence on science and philosophy for several centuries, and was widely considered second only to Aristotle in knowledge.

Lived between: 870-950 CE

Alhazen (Ibn al-Haytham)

Optics and the development of the scientific method. His work profoundly influenced both Islamic and Western science. Authored the influential "Book of Optics" (Kitab al-Manazir); pioneering experimental scientific method; major advances in vision science and mathematics

Lived between: 965–c. 1040/1041 CE

No. of books written: Attributed with over 200 works; about 96 known, 50 extant, 14 on optics

Contributions to: Optics, mathematics, astronomy, physics, philosophy, medicine, engineering, psychology

Ibn Khaldun

Foundational Thinker in Sociology, History, and Economics

Lived between: 1332–1406 CE (1 Ramadan 732 AH – 25 Ramadan 808 AH)

No. of books written: Major works: "al-Muqaddimah" (introduction to "Kitab al-‘Ibar"), "al-Ta‘rif", "Lubab al-Muhashshal fi Usul al-Din", and others; total number is debated, but his main legacy is the Muqaddimah and its associated volumes

Contributions to: Sociology, History, Economics, Political science, Philosophy, Education, Communication, Historiography, Religious studies
